Waiting perhaps?

Marc was already there. His back against the leather couch, slowly sipping beer. He had his headphones in. From time to time you could see him nodding to a rhythm only he would know. Unapproachable. Waiting perhaps? From his features you could tell that he is not from around here. Your first guess would have been Italian. If he would have had a cigar in his hand, not a cigarette, there would be no doubt. Definitely Italian. For those who know him, he was half Algerian half French, for those who didn’t, he was Danish. He’s been living here for more than 25 years. He can call himself that. The only thing that gives away is his strong accent. An interesting mix of Arabic and French

”1-2...10-11 pints”

He comes to Nørreport Bodega every friday afternoon for to shake off a long week at work with a couple of beers, looking for peace of mind and relaxation in a place that he considered private.

Round face, round glasses, chin dimple and receding hairline...Just picture it. He would have made a great silly comedy actor. The otherwise dim lighting in the room would have hidden his features, but the lamp from above his table accentuated them.

Leaving aside the things he could have been, the professions he could have had, we’re turning back to reality, and his supply division cubicle from UNICEF. He talks with passion about his work and how everytime you want to send out supplies to countries in need, you have to filter it through Copenhagen first. “ For example, there is a disaster in Bolivia, they want syringes. You can ask here [Copenhagen], the supplier, and we pack them, but there are so many scenarios.”

“ Together, against what?! Mafia.”

Marc talks about an ongoing revolution in Algeria against the upcoming presidential elections. “Every friday at least 3 million people go out into the streets to protest against the system.”
He himself flew out to Paris a week ago to take part in an organised peaceful demonstration and proudly shared pictures and videos of that. He was excited to be a part of change. He considered this approach “something new, something different”, because no matter the background, religion or sexual orientation, algerians have the same scope, fighting together peacefully, against their country’s corrupt system, that he describes as “the mafia”. 

The Algerian Revolution
The Algerian protests, also known as the Revolution of Smiles, started on February 16th 2019, after the current president - Abdelaziz Bouteflika - announced his candidacy for a fith presidential tern and signed a statement. Tensions, building up since the beginning Bouteflika's rule, rose and the people of Algeria came together in some of the major urban centers. The peaceful protest attracted international media attention and provoked reactions of numourous heads of states. It drove the military to insist on Bouteflika's resignation, which took place on the 2nd of April 2019 and in the following months, several arrests occured, among which Bouteflika's younger brother - Said.

“Ça va?” 

The political talk was disrupted by Olivier, the awaited man, Marc’s friend. As he approached, couple “ça va’s” were exchanged between them, as well as the iconic french triple cheek kiss.

Olivier was born in Congo. He travelled and lived in almost every continent, now pursuing a bachelor in Copenhagen at DTU. He first came in Denmark at age 11, although he lived some many places after, he still came back and is proud to call Copenhagen home. Olivier has two kids here and a norvegian girlfriend, he was sharing that he would never move to norway for a girl given his children here.

Olivier stood tall, having an admirable confidence. His golden watch case almost covered his whole hand. His rings set him aside from many men; golden, thick, bold and full of crystals.
“He’s a millionaire! He’s a millionaire!” Marc exclaims.
A friend in belgium made his jewelry.
“It’s diamonds! He’s a millionaire!” Marc insists.
They were definitely not diamonds.

“It’s now or never!”

Out of silence Olivier catches Marc’s attention: “c'est ce soir”. They further explained that tonight is the biggest possible Eurojackpot amount to be won , “its now or never”. The win was 1 million euros.
“We have to play! I am tired to be poor!” said Marc.
If he would win, he’d love to share and give away his winnings. More plans about tonight’s game were shared in French.

Empty Bottles

"Can you stop?!"
